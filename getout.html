GetOut — Emergency Evacuation Decision Support

A research-driven project focused on improving evacuation communication, coordination, and decision-making for vulnerable communities.

The Problem

Emergency evacuations often suffer from poor communication, fragmented tools, and lack of coordination between stakeholders. Critical information such as when to evacuate, where to go, and how to get there is not consistently delivered in a timely or accessible way.

Stakeholders

  • Indigenous communities
  • Emergency response teams
  • Government and leadership
  • Host communities
  • Vulnerable populations

Key Insights

  • Communication breakdowns are a major failure point
  • Single-channel communication is unreliable
  • Real-time coordination is limited
  • Accessibility is often overlooked

Solution Direction

GetOut is designed as a decision-support system that improves evacuation planning through real-time communication, structured guidance, and improved coordination between stakeholders.
